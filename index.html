Projects

Artistry Canvas

The Artistry Canvas allows you to express your imagination with a free-hand drawing tool that transforms your digital device into an artistic playground. Change colors, adjust pen sizes, and use various brush types to bring your vision to life.

View Project

Shape Master

Shape Master is your go-to tool for creating precise geometric shapes. Whether you're designing patterns, logos, or schematic diagrams, this feature allows you to easily select and draw shapes such as circles, squares, rectangles, and triangles.

View Project

Letter Artistry

Letter Artistry is an interactive platform that allows users to draw alphabets directly on a digital canvas. This innovative tool recognizes hand-drawn letters in real time, transforming simple strokes into corresponding alphabets.

View Project

Clash of Hands

Clash of Hands is an interactive game that lets you play Rock, Paper, Scissors, Spock, and Lizard with an AI opponent using real-time hand gestures. Make your move with your hands, and the game will determine the winner in a fun, dynamic way.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of this project?

+

This project aims to provide users with a dynamic and interactive platform to express creativity through various tools, including free-hand drawing, shapes, and emoji-based hand gestures game.

How can I contribute to this project?

+

You can contribute by sharing feedback, reporting bugs, or even collaborating on new features through our GitHub repository or community forum.

Is the project free to use?

+

Yes, this project is open-source and free to use for everyone. Feel free to explore the tools and enhance your creativity!

What technologies are used in this project?

+

The project is built using a combination of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for the frontend, along with OpenCV, TensorFlow, and MediaPipe for gesture recognition and hand tracking functionalities.

What is the difference between the various drawing tools?

+

Each tool has unique properties: the pencil is precise for fine lines, the brush has soft strokes for painting, the crayon creates textured lines, and the calligraphy pen simulates artistic lettering.
